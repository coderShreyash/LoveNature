Pine Tree

Description:

  1. Pines are some of the best-known plants around the world.
  2. They possess huge economic importance through the timber trade and are easily identifiable due to their characteristic cone-shaped growth form and needle-like leaves.
  3. Pines usually have male and female cones on the same tree (monoecious). They fall as soon as they have shed their pollen.
  4. The pines are a family of around 250 woody, seed-producing plants.
  5. They include conifers such as cedars, spruces, firs and pines.
  6. Of all the conifers, the pines have one of the largest distributions although they are found almost entirely in the Northern Hemisphere.
  7. Many species have the ability to withstand burning.
  8. Their thick layer of bark helps to protect the tree during fires and prevent burning of vulnerable woody tissue.

Found in:

  1. Pines live in almost the entire Northern Hemisphere.
  2. In North America, they live in the southern part of Arctic to Nicaragua and Hispaniola.
  3. In Europe, they live in areas from Portugal and Scotland to Russia.
  4. In Asia, they live in areas from Russia to Japan and the Philippines.
  5. They also live in the Himalayas, some northern parts of Africa, and north of Majorca (Mallorca) on the rugged mountains.

Uses in Forestry:

  1. Timber and paper industries around the world are built around the growth of planted pine forests.
  2. Timber is an essential building material all around the world, both in first and third world countries; and paper has been used for various purposes for hundreds of years.
  3. Pines are the dominant trees grown in the majority of countries for both the timber and paper industries and have been critical in both of these multi-billion dollar industries.
